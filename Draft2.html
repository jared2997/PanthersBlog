Mock Draft 2.0


This is my second mock draft for the Panthers before the draft in April and just like the last one, I drafted based on needs for the Panthers.


2.33: WR Troy Franklin, Oregon



Troy Franklin is a wide receiver from Oregon and stands 6 foot 3 and weighs 187 pounds. He is a very fast receiver and is projected to be a late first rounder and luckily I got him in the second round. His stats for last season were 81 catches for 1383 yards and 14 touchdowns and averaged 17.1 yards per catch. That is what Bryce Young needs, a speedy receiver to get open and with Franklin to do just that, it could be a deadly duo for this team in the future.


3.65: TE Ja'Tavion Sanders, Texas



Sanders, a tight end out of Texas stands 6 foot 4 243 pounds is more of a blocking threat than receiver but can get open and be a safety blanket for Bryce Young if no one else is open. His stats for last year were 45 catches for 682 yards and 2 touchdowns. He could help in the blocking game with Tommy Tremble to help the running backs produce more than they did this year.


4.101: EDGE Jonah Elliss, Utah



Elliss is a defensive end out of Utah and only played 9 games and had 23 solo tackles, 12 sacks, and 1 forced fumble. He also is 6 foot 2 and weighs 246 pounds. Him and Brian Burns can be a deadly duo if both are played the right way and Evero has another freak on the defensive line with Elliss on the opposite side of Burns. And not only that, the Panthers could always use more defensive end depth in case of any injury for next season.


5.140: IOL Dominick Puni, Kansas



Puni is an interior offensive lineman from the University of Kansas and stands 6 foot 4 and weighs 323 pounds. He is a well-versed and skilled run-blocker and has amazing mobility to aim low and drive the defense back for the running back to get through and is an amazing double teamer in blocking. His pass blocking is controlled and wide-bodied presence on the edge and is a quick and firm anchor to protect the QB. This is what the Panthers need a big guy to help give Bryce Young time to throw the ball which the Panthers failed to do last year.


5.165: LB Marist Liufau, Norte Dame



Liufau is a linebacker from the University of Notre Dame and stands 6 foot 2 and weighs 235 pounds. He had 44 tackles, 3 sacks, and 1 forced fumble and 1 fumble recovery. He is a perfect fit for this Panthers defense if we lose Frankie Luvu and if Shaq Thompson does not come back until later in the season. He is not afraid to be physical and can get sacks if he is played the right way.


6.179: RB Dillon Johnson, Washington



Johnson is a running back from the University of Washington and was a big part of the success that Washington had this season. He stands 6 foot and weighs 218 pounds. His stats for the season was 233 carries and 1195 yards and had 16 touchdowns and also had 24 catches for 190 yards. He is a swift runner and hits the gaps hard which paired with Chubba Hubbard and Miles Sanders could be the future RB2 or RB3 for the Panthers for this upcoming year.
